D20 Game Rules

Initial Setup

  • Player Order: Each player rolls a 20-sided die (d20). The highest roll goes first. In case of a tie, tied players roll again.
  • Character Creation: All characters start at level 5. Players choose a class (with Game Master approval). Each class comes with a base skill. Characters start with one weapon that suits their class.

Turn Structure

The game is played in scenes. Each scene represents one complete turn rotation.

  • Each player gets one movement and one action per turn
  • Actions can include: attacking, using a skill, interacting with the environment, or other GM-approved actions
  • When multiple players are in the same scene, they may interact with each other
  • Once all players have completed their actions in a scene, the turn order continues with the next scene

Leveling Up

  • Characters can gain up to 3 levels in a single scene
  • Leveling up can result from:
    • Winning battles or combat encounters
    • Completing major story events or quests
    • Finding and using powerful artifacts
  • As characters level up, their health, damage, and defense all scale accordingly

Inventory Management

  • Each character can hold up to 3 items
  • Certain special items may allow characters to hold more than 3 items
  • All items must be:
    • Appropriate for the character's class
    • Approved by the Game Master

Game Rules & Endgame

  • Class Restrictions: Characters' weapons must suit their class unless the GM decides otherwise
  • Class Changes: Characters cannot change their class unless the GM specifically allows it
  • Artifact Rules: Once an artifact's description and effects are determined by the GM, they cannot be changed
  • Starting Location: All characters begin in the same location unless specified by the GM
  • Endgame: The game concludes once the designated end goal is achieved. The end goal is defined by the GM and can involve:
    • Completing a major quest or mission
    • Defeating a final boss or antagonist
    • Reaching a specific location or destination

Game Master's Role

  • The GM is responsible for setting scenes and describing the game world
  • The GM defines challenges and obstacles
  • The GM arbitrates all rules
  • The GM has the final say on all game-related decisions, including interpreting rules and resolving disputes

Role-Playing & Interaction

Players are strongly encouraged to:

  • Role-play their characters and speak in character when appropriate
  • Interact creatively with the game world
  • Collaborate with other players to create interesting stories
  • Suggest creative solutions to problems posed by the GM

Remember: D20 is designed to be flexible. If a situation isn't covered by these rules, work with your GM to come up with a fair solution that keeps the story moving and keeps everyone having fun!
